Research statement

Tue 31 May 2016

In revision… <!— Designing civil systems which are more economic, sustainable, and display adequate performance and reliability in a highly uncertain environment is what permanently drives innovation in civil engineering. Quantification and incorporation of the effects of uncertainty in the analysis and design process of such systems, in a rational manner, is the next big challenge yet to be solved in civil engineering.

Accounting for parametric and forcing uncertainty demands either solving a system of nonlinear (Stratonovich) stochastic partial differential equations, or performing Monte-Carlo simulations with regular, albeit millions of, deterministic models. This increases the size of the system to be analyzed and the generated output data by orders of magnitude, especially for problems of practical interest. The only way to meet the computational demands of such a large model is through high performance computing.

My current research is, therefore, focused on designing, implementing, and maintaining a modeling system for large scale nonlinear stochastic finite element problems, the UCD Earthquake-Soil-Structure interaction simulator (ESSI Simulator). Additionally, but no less important, is to provide an effective way to analyze the large amount of data produced by each simulation.

Among my contributions to the ESSI Simulator code, I have created a domain specific language for modeling that serves as input to the program. I have also increased overall performance of certain key bottleneck processes by using C++ “template meta-programming” techniques, specifically in plasticity computations. One of my current short term goals is to design and implement a data storage system for the large output (order of hundreds of gigabytes to several terabytes) that the code generates.

These contributions are all geared towards providing a fertile ground for my long term research goal: extending ESSI to incorporate nonlinear stochastic time domain analysis capability. Adopting analysis and design technique which explicitly deal with uncertainty will ultimately change the way modeling is approached and understood in civil engineering, while providing more meaningful information to decision makers about how their decisions can affect a system both from a performance and lifetime cost point of view.>

José A. Abell – May 2014
