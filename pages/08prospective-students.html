PhD Students

I invite prospective PhD students to consider joining our PhD program at Universidad de los Andes.

My research style is the application of high-performance computing, programming, and visualization techniques across a diverse range of areas, including:

  • Strong motion seismology
  • Seismic wave propagation
  • Advanced constitutive modeling
  • Nonlinear dynamic finite element modeling
  • Soil-structure interaction modeling and simulation
  • Fluid-structure interaction modeling and simulation
  • Fluid-soil-structure interaction modeling and simulation
  • Thermal modeling
  • Underground flow modeling
  • Physics-based/informed risk assessment
  • Structural life-cycle prediction
  • Offshore/onshore wind applications
  • Scouring processes

These fields are increasingly relevant to the global challenges we face today, and by joining our program, you will have the opportunity to contribute significantly to our understanding and our response to these challenges. Applicants are expected to have interests in one or more of these fields as well as programming skills in a high-level language (Python, Matlab, Julia, etc.), ideally in a low-level language (C, C++, Fortran) or at least the drive to learn one. New research skills I want to develop are data-driven modelling through sensor data (in collaboration with ASDEA Hardware) and design of mechanical devices for diverse applications through modeling and 3-D printing.

Information about the PhD program at Universidad de los Andes, including the application process, can be found here. Although the program is in Spanish, we welcome applications from English-speaking candidates.

Both Chilean and international students are required to apply for funding through scholarships from the Chilean Agency of Research and Development ANID. These scholarships are awarded on the basis of academic merit and potential for research excellence. The application process involves writing a research proposal, with my sponsoring of your application, where we propose a joint research plan.

Master's Program

With the recent opening of our Master of Science (MS) and PhD programs, which is oriented to research in the field of civil infrastructure systems, students are now being sought to pursue these degrees by doing research with me. More information on the program can be found here.

Completion of the master's/PhD program typically leads to the publication of a scientific article (paper) in a top journal (Q1 ranking on JCR Thompson Reuters) in the fields of computational mechanics, earthquake engineering, nonlinear/probabilistic dynamics, to name a few, which would boost the students CV immensely.

National or international students interested in pursuing such a degree should contact me directly.
