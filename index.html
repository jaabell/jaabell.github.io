About

Sun 08 February 2015

I am a chilean professor working at Universidad de los Andes within the Civil Infrastructure Systems group.

My research focuses on modeling and simulation of nonlinear soils and structures under earthquake loading (see [RealESSI Simulator][]) both using deterministic and probabilistic (stochastic) models.

A major goal for me is to leverage the use of high-performance computing as the enabling technology that will allow the profession to move forward in terms of the power of analysis available to engineers.

Simulation of a surface wave (Rayleigh)

Sun 30 November 2014

This simulation, done in the UCD ESSI simulator and visualized in VisIt using VisIt-ESSI plugin, shows the passage of a surface wave (Rayleigh wave). The simulation consists on a surface impact on an elastic domain of 900m by 1800m depth, and a shear wave velocity of 1000km/s. Elliptical-retrograde motion can be seen as an illustration of Rayleigh waves.
